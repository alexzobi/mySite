But Why?

Such a simple question. And yet, how often do we take the time to ask?

For me, the answer was "not enough." For too long, I operated under the impression that success was what others had defined it as for me. Success was a big house with the perfect family to fill it. Success was a 45 year career. Success was money. The more money, the more success.

Depression is a funny thing. It can sneak up on you without you even noticing. It can move into your brain like an unwelcomed tennant, impossible to evict. It can change the way you eat. The way you sleep. The way you breathe.

But Why is the story of my personal journey to redefining success. To removing the sadness in my life and discovering what it means to truly be happy.

It isn't mind-blowing. It isn't revolutionary. It's just me. But maybe, it's also you.
